Week 1

Scoping Your Rust+AI System

Choose and scope a project that is ambitious but shippable in 6 weeks.

  • Define user, problem, success criteria, and constraints.
  • Pick model/provider strategy: LLM, classical ML, or external API.
  • Lock in the integration surface and what's out of scope.
Week 2

Designing AI-Era Systems in Rust

System architecture: Rust services, data pipeline, model inference boundaries, observability.

  • Design service boundaries between Rust and AI components.
  • Plan data flow, state management, and the concurrency model.
  • Identify failure modes and observability points for AI calls.
Week 3

Implementation I — End-to-End Path

Get the core data and inference path running end-to-end.

  • Stand up the request → AI → response pipeline.
  • Wire up minimal but real persistence and caching where needed.
  • Validate the system against real or realistic data.
Week 4

Implementation II — Reliability & Performance

Robustness, performance, concurrency, error handling, retries, monitoring.

  • Harden timeouts, backoff, retries, and error handling.
  • Add logging, metrics, and tracing around AI calls and Rust services.
  • Think through security and basic abuse / guardrail patterns.
Week 5

Hardening, Scaling & Deployment

Load, resilience, security, and pragmatic deployment options.

  • Choose a deployment approach: container, serverless, or on-prem.
  • Design a runbook for monitoring and debugging in prod-ish environments.
  • Pressure-test the system before final review.
Week 6

Portfolio & Opportunity Packaging

Turn the project into a written case study, diagrams, and an interview narrative.

  • Write a concise case study: problem, design, tradeoffs, outcome.
  • Prepare diagrams and a 5–10 minute "walk me through this" script.
  • Map the project to roles, a promotion path, or a client offer.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior AI/ML experience?
No formal AI/ML background required. We'll cover the integration patterns and model-choice tradeoffs you need to ship a serious system. If you've never touched ML, that's fine — you'll lean into LLM/API-driven designs first.
What level of Rust do I need?
You should be comfortable writing Rust day-to-day — ownership, traits, error handling, and basic async/concurrency. This is alumni-only by design so we can move fast and stay system-focused.
What kinds of projects are appropriate?
Anything with a real user, a real data flow, and a real AI surface — RAG services, agentic backends, retrieval pipelines, inference proxies, ML feature stores, AI-augmented dev tools. Your week-1 1-on-1 is dedicated to scoping the right one.
What if I can't attend a live session?
All sessions are recorded. You'll still get value from async Discord support and your two 1-on-1s, but we recommend at least one of the two weekly calls live — that's where the cohort momentum lives.
Can my project be for my current employer?
Yes — and many students choose to. We'll help you scope it so it's both shippable in 6 weeks and a legitimate portfolio asset you can talk about externally (within whatever constraints you have).
Is this about getting an AI job, a promotion, or building a product?
All three. The final 1-on-1 and the Portfolio & Interview Pack are explicitly built around mapping your project to roles, promotion narrative, or a client/product pitch — your call.
